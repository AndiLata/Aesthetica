What is Aesthetica?

Aesthetica is a community power tool. It exists because affordable housing residents are constantly told that their surroundings don't matter — that basic shelter is enough. Decades of research in urban studies, neuroscience, and the sociology of place say otherwise.

When communities control the aesthetics of their space — when they author it, vote on it, and own it — something changes. Belonging deepens. Recovery accelerates. Organizing becomes possible. This is aesthetic justice.

Aesthetica gives residents two creative paths and one democratic mechanism. Drop your vision. Let the community ratify it. Take the mandate into any room that needs to hear it.

01

Residents are the authors

AI is a tool — one option among many. The vision, the intent, the choice to use it or reject it: that belongs to the person who lives here.

02

Community ratification is democracy

A single voice is a suggestion. Fifteen votes is a mandate. The threshold turns individual vision into collective will.

03

Neglect is not neutral

Choosing not to invest in a community's appearance is a political act. Aesthetica makes that visible and builds the case for change.
